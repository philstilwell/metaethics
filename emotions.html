Is this a mental-health assessment?

No. It maps philosophical and practical roles for emotions. It does not diagnose distress or tell anyone to stop feeling on command. Severe or persistent distress may deserve appropriate human or professional support beyond this site.

The main lesson

Accountability can survive without treating suffering as intrinsically deserved.

Harm can be named, trust defended, protest heard, repair required, and commitments expressed. The remaining task is to shape emotion around those purposes without preserving prejudice, manipulation, permanent stigma, or punishment for its own sake.
