Does “no direct standing” mean “destroy it”?

No. An entity can lack direct standing and still be protected because people value it, because a policy has good effects, or because caution is useful. The option says only that the entity itself supplies no direct claim on the fixed facts.

The main lesson

A boundary can be defended without pretending that inclusion and equal weight are the same.

Experience, agency, interests, relationships, membership, discovered status, and constructed rules can overlap or separate. A complete view says which features are sufficient, how uncertainty is handled, and why exclusions do not merely copy familiar prejudice.
