# Editorial Style Sheet

## Governing aim

The prose should feel like an intelligent expedition: concrete before abstract, rigorous without ceremonial stiffness, skeptical without sneering, and witty without treating the subject or reader as foolish. The reader is a collaborator carrying a notebook through Port Meridian, not a spectator receiving doctrine.

## Language and punctuation

- Use American English: *favor*, *judgment*, *toward*, *center*, *defense*, and *skeptic*.
- Use the serial comma.
- Use typographic quotation marks and apostrophes in publication text.
- Set em dashes closed—without surrounding spaces. Use en dashes for numerical ranges in publication output.
- Prefer contractions in narrative and dialogue when they improve naturalness; avoid them in formal definitions when expansion prevents ambiguity.
- Spell out whole numbers from one through nine in prose. Use numerals for 10 and above, chapter numbers, measurements, probabilities, clock times, version numbers, table entries, and deliberately enumerated systems.
- Italicize words under discussion and book titles. Use quotation marks for direct speech, proposed clauses, and short expressions whose wording is being examined.
- Use sentence case for internal headings. Chapter and part titles remain title case.

## Project terms

- **Candidate System** and **Debt Ledger** name the reader’s recurring artifacts and remain capitalized.
- **Inspection Report** is capitalized when it names one of the six numbered landings.
- *Pressure case*, *bridge*, *selector*, *rotation test*, and *grounding partition* are lowercase except in headings.
- **Objective moral fact** and **objective moral system** are not interchangeable. A verdict is not a system.
- **Direct standing**, comparative weight, and final outcome remain distinct.
- **Aim-independent reason** is preferred when the text means a practical reason whose existence does not depend on the addressed agent’s aims. Do not use *objective reason* unless the ambiguity with fact-relative reasons is being discussed.
- **Basic desert** means deserved benefit or suffering independently of further protective, reparative, communicative, deterrent, or rehabilitative purpose.
- **Primitive** means a practical starting response or concern, not something simple, infallible, universal, fixed, or beyond criticism.

## Voice and rhythm

- Address the reader directly when asking for a choice, revision, or exercise. Use *we* for an inquiry the author and reader are conducting together.
- Lead theoretical sections with a case, question, or strongest motivating insight.
- Vary paragraph length. A short paragraph should land an inference or change the room, not merely decorate the page.
- Preserve the book’s dry wit, especially when it exposes hidden premises, bureaucratic inflation, or overconfident abstractions. Do not make a joke at a victim’s expense.
- Avoid throat-clearing phrases such as *it is important to note*. State the point.
- Use *clearly*, *obviously*, and *simply* only when the word itself is under inspection or the claim really is secured by stipulated facts.
- Prefer one stable metaphor at a time. Construction, bridge, ledger, inspection, map, translation, and maze may interact; do not add a new metaphor merely to enliven one sentence.

## Reader orientation

- Use **Where this chapter is headed** once, after the opening scene, to state the chapter's task and cumulative significance in plain language.
- Use **Purpose** on reusable worksheets and policy forms to state what the reader is producing and what the result leaves open.
- Prefer direct formulations such as *This distinction matters because...* or *This step establishes...* over vague promises that a point will become important later.
- Do not annotate a section whose heading, opening, and closing already make its role clear. Signposts should reduce uncertainty, not create a second book running beside the first.
- A closing transition should name the debt or question inherited by the next chapter rather than merely announcing that the next chapter exists.

## Philosophical fairness

- Present the insight a view protects before applying pressure.
- Distinguish a theory family from any one historical philosopher. Do not attribute the book’s synthetic formulation to an author unless the note supplies a source.
- Do not treat a stopping point as false merely because it is basic. Ask what content, scope, comparison rule, authority, and access the stopping point contains.
- Do not infer nonexistence from failed access, disagreement from falsity, causal explanation from debunking, social construction from worthlessness, or non-realism from nihilism.
- State whether an objection establishes contradiction, underdetermination, an explanatory debt, an epistemic burden, practical inadequacy, or only the price of a view.
- A fully informed rejecter is a pressure device, not a claim that most actual opponents lack compassion or shared commitments.

## Cases and continuity

- Recurring characters retain their established functions: Asha—reform; Iona—role and impartiality; Sela—evidence and constrained agency; Ren—identity and autonomy; Nara—promise and loyalty; Kest—coherent rejection; Vale—power and coercion; Niko—responsibility and emotion.
- Port Meridian is a continuous fictional setting. Institutional details may be stylized, but a callback must point to a case that has actually appeared.
- Hold stipulated facts fixed until the exercise explicitly varies one. Do not rescue a favored verdict by inventing a motive, danger, law, or consequence.
- Fictional cases are original unless the notes identify a classic structure. Avoid naming a classic case in a way that falsely suggests the exact version is historically canonical.

## Notes and bibliography

- Use chapter-level notes and further reading so the main journey remains uncluttered.
- Use Chicago-style bibliographic entries, with DOI or stable URL for journal articles and online works when available.
- Primary works anchor historical and interpretive claims. Reference works orient terminology and routes into larger debates.
- No source is cited as endorsing the book’s cumulative conclusion unless it actually does.

## Publication boundary

- The published book does not mention the website, software, labs, diagnostics, profiles, scores, configuration files, or source-code implementation.
- Internal editorial and research documents may name those materials when maintaining conceptual alignment.
