# Claim and Source Audit

## Audit rule

The manuscript is an original synthetic argument, not a history of philosophy. Most claims therefore need one of three treatments:

1. **Primary-source anchor** when a recognizable philosophical position or argument is presented.
2. **Reference orientation** when a beginner-facing distinction maps a large literature.
3. **No external citation** when the passage is an original fictional case, stipulated inference, exercise instruction, or transparent logical distinction.

Chapter-level notes are preferable to frequent superscripts because the narrative repeatedly combines ideas from several traditions. The notes must not imply that one named author endorses the book’s exact formulation or conclusion.

## Source-balance finding

The repository’s original reading room is strongest on non-realism, genealogy, post-error practice, responsibility skepticism, reasons, standing, evidence, and value conflict. The publication bibliography therefore adds primary or book-length representatives of robust moral realism, Kantian constructivism, contractualism, consequentialism, virtue ethics, care ethics, divine-nature ethics, natural law, and political construction.

The principal balancing additions are David Enoch, Russ Shafer-Landau, Terence Cuneo, Derek Parfit, Christine Korsgaard, T. M. Scanlon, John Rawls, Immanuel Kant, Aristotle, John Stuart Mill, Virginia Held, Robert Merrihew Adams, and John Finnis.

## Empirical-claim finding

The book uses empirical examples primarily as stipulated cases. Where it discusses pain, trauma, development, prediction, institutional effects, or cultural formation, the prose makes conditional or methodological claims rather than reporting contested quantitative findings. No fabricated statistic or empirical study citation was found.

Before commercial publication, any new real-world case study or numerical claim must receive a source-specific factual review. Fictional probabilities and counts should remain visibly inside imagined cases.

## Quotation and originality finding

The manuscript contains no unattributed quotation from the audited sources. Its recurring cases, Port Meridian setting, Candidate System, Debt Ledger, Inspection Reports, and Civic Stakes Brief are original project structures. Familiar thought-experiment shapes are reformulated and used as pressure devices rather than represented as historical quotations.
