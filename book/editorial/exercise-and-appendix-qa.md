# Exercise and Appendix Usability Audit

## Audit standard

The exercises are part of the cumulative argument. A reader should be able to perform every construction without guessing what is fixed, what must be supplied, what inference is permitted, or where a revision belongs. The appendices should work independently after the main journey while preserving the book's distinctions and restrained conclusion.

## Main-text exercise coverage

Every chapter contains at least one reader action, bounded pressure case, construction artifact, audit, or inspection. The sequence is cumulative rather than quiz-like:

| Movement | Principal reader work | Durable artifact |
|---|---|---|
| Prologue and Part I | State the first principle, define objectivity, declare the meaning rule, choose success conditions, and separate layers of disagreement | Specification sheet and first Debt Ledger entries |
| Part II | Draw standing, define verdict objects, select values, write generation rules, choose a comparison method, and test exceptions | First complete Candidate System |
| Part III | Classify reasons, test idealization, draft commitment and construction warrants, confront aim-independent reasons, and separate pressure from obligation | Authority matrix and explicit fork |
| Part IV | Write an epistemic charter, calibrate intuition, trace genealogy, classify disagreement, separate factual input from evaluative selection, and specify correction | Access and correction protocol |
| Part V | Give six major theory families and a hybrid their strongest constitutions | Candidate constitutions and comparative inspection |
| Part VI | Build criticism, public-code, coercion, responsibility, and emotion warrants; then conduct the grounding audit | Final Debt Ledger and Inspection Report |
| Part VII | Choose a language policy, inventory survivors, name primitives, and complete a public policy brief | Language charter and Civic Stakes Brief |

The chapters use varied headings so the journey does not sound like a workbook wearing a trench coat. The action itself remains explicit. No chapter requires a conclusion before the distinction needed to reach it has been introduced.

## Appendix inventory and integrity

| Appendix | Verified contents | Usability finding |
|---|---:|---|
| A — Candidate System Workbook | 19 numbered stages plus cover sheet | Reconstructs the full cumulative audit in the order the concepts are introduced. Earlier answers are preserved and revision types are defined. |
| B — Pressure Cases | 45 numbered cards plus blank card | Standard cards distinguish fixed facts, exact question, variations, and inference tested. Four composite cards state their fixed conditions as numbered miniature cases; the introduction now explains the variation. |
| C — Common Repairs and Their Debts | 18 numbered repairs plus blank repair card | Each entry gives the strongest reading, accomplishment, debt, pressure questions, and an honest narrower form. This prevents caricature and makes a repair recordable. |
| D — Civic Stakes Brief | 14 numbered sections, final record, independent review, and public summary | Matches the fourteen-part brief in Chapter 41. Headings were synchronized to remove small naming differences. |
| E — Glossary and Position Map | Core project vocabulary, major metaethical positions, reasons, standing, first-order families, responsibility, emotion, and power | Definitions are beginner-facing and keep dimensions separate; the compact map warns that labels do not answer every axis. |
| F — Limits of the Conclusion | Target, grounding partition, seven findings, conclusion, non-conclusions, epistemic stances, and revision conditions | Functions as a formal guardrail against metaphysical overclaim, practical nihilism, and illicit transfer from failed access to nonexistence. |

## Cross-appendix checks

- Workbook stages correspond to the construction order in Parts I–VII.
- Pressure-case part labels follow the seven-part structure and preserve bounded-inference instructions.
- Repair entries name relocated debts rather than treating a rhetorical restatement as payment.
- Civic Stakes Brief section 14 records both support and dissent; it does not manufacture community consensus.
- Glossary terminology matches the editorial style sheet, including *direct standing*, *aim-independent reason*, *basic desert*, and *primitive*.
- The limits appendix preserves the central result as an earned construction failure, not a proof of logical impossibility or actual nonexistence.

## Production requirements

The print assembly must preserve the usability that exists in the source:

1. start each numbered workbook worksheet on a fresh page;
2. prevent a pressure card's heading from being stranded at a page bottom;
3. repeat table headers after page breaks;
4. keep checkboxes and writing rules large enough to mark by hand;
5. allow long policy tables to continue rather than shrinking them below readable type;
6. make the workbook and Civic Stakes Brief easy to photocopy or print independently;
7. visually inspect pages with the widest standing, authority, power, policy, and measurement tables.

These requirements are layout constraints, not optional polish. They will be checked against the rendered PDF.
