# Contents and Reading Routes

This book is one cumulative expedition. The exercises are part of the route: they make you specify the system, choose among repairs, and record what each choice costs. The straight-through path is therefore the recommended one.

The focused routes below are available when you want to revisit a particular question. They are shortcuts, not philosophical camps, personality types, or substitutes for the whole construction.

## Focused route: The case for and against objective authority

Read the Prologue; Chapters 1–5, 12–24, and 37–39; then Appendix F. This route follows the specification, authority, access, and final grounding argument while leaving most first-order theory tournaments for later.

## Focused route: Building public policy after the search

Read Chapters 5–11, 22–24, 29, and 32–41; then complete Appendix D. This route begins with the anatomy of disagreement and ends with a public method for evidence, standing, tradeoffs, power, safeguards, review, and repair.

## Focused route: Responsibility, blame, and moral emotion

Read Chapters 3, 7, 21, and 35–41; then consult the responsibility and emotion entries in Appendix E. This route separates agency and accountability from cosmic payback, then asks what guilt, shame, resentment, indignation, gratitude, and pride can still do.
