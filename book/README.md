# The Objective Morality Project

Working subtitle: **A Guided Attempt to Build a Moral System That Everyone Has Reason to Obey**

This directory contains the foundation for a new book derived from the conceptual work in this repository. The published manuscript will not mention websites, labs, diagnostics, software, scores, or the suite. It will stand on its own as a continuous philosophical journey.

## Governing promise

The book invites the reader to attempt a construction:

> Build a coherent, objective, knowable, and action-guiding moral system whose authority does not depend on anyone already valuing its conclusions.

The reader begins with a reasonable expectation of success. Each part supplies a component that seems necessary: a domain, objects of judgment, values, meanings, statuses, rules, reasons, procedures for conflict, empirical inputs, responsibility practices, and a method of revision. Each apparent repair is then pressure-tested.

The cumulative conclusion is deliberately narrower and stronger than a dismissive slogan:

> No candidate examined earns all the properties expected of an objective moral system. Each either depends on human or divine stances, imports an unsupported evaluative bridge, leaves its decisive selectors brute, becomes indeterminate when values conflict, or secures determinacy by surrendering universality, accessibility, revisability, or humane application.

This is a conclusion about what the construction project has established. The book will not pretend to have deductively disproved every logically imaginable moral fact.

## Reader experience

The manuscript uses four recurring devices:

1. **The Candidate System** — the reader writes and revises a compact moral constitution.
2. **The Debt Ledger** — every claim of authority creates an explanatory debt that must be paid without circularity.
3. **Pressure Cases** — bounded cases hold tempting escape routes fixed and expose one structural problem at a time.
4. **Inspection Reports** — each part records what survived, what was surrendered, and what remains unsupported.

Exercises are part of the argument, not optional comprehension quizzes. The reader must choose definitions, rank values, confront counterexamples, state exception rules, and decide which aspiration to weaken when a repair cannot preserve everything.

## Current drafting status

The book architecture is complete at the chapter level. It currently contains forty-one chapters, including dedicated treatments of epistemic normativity, direct standing, value comparison, and moral emotions after desert. The prologue and all thirty-one chapters of Parts I–V are drafted, totaling approximately 92,100 words. The reader has moved from the blank constitution through the first complete Candidate System, audited its possible sources of objective authority and access, and then given consequentialism, deontology, virtue and care, received-order theories, constructivism, local or plural standards, and hybrid systems their strongest hearings. Inspection Report V preserves the human concern and practical contribution of every candidate while recording the repair cost and remaining objective debt. Part VI can now test the last proposed rescues and state the general result without reducing the preceding theories to caricatures.

The working title is intentionally invitational rather than conclusory. It can be replaced without changing the architecture.
