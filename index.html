Read this carefully

Coherence is not vindication.

A system can be logically consistent and still be false, cruel, based on bad facts, unfairly imposed, unrealistic, or badly motivated. This score only checks whether the system clearly explains its boundaries, reasoning steps, evidence, and ways to correct mistakes.

Based on Phil Stilwell’s field guide to moral systems and the parsimonious non-realist framework.

Runs entirely in your browser. No AI, account, or tracking.

Method & limits

A solvency audit, not a truth machine

The test does not assume that moral facts exist outside human minds and practices. It treats “moral” and “immoral” as labels that need explanation. A system must say who counts, what is being judged, what matters, what its words mean, and how it reaches a conclusion.

The thirteen tests check who counts, what is judged, what matters, what words and verdicts mean, how values become rules, which reasons are offered, whether like cases are treated alike, how conflicts are handled, whether facts are checked, whether people can use the system, where it came from, and how it can change. Clear rules in this page score the answers. Nothing is sent to a server, and no AI reads the answers.

A high score means the proposed system has made more of its architecture visible and stable. It does not establish objective moral facts, prove that the system is humane, or authorize anyone to impose it.
