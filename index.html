Read this carefully

A mixed profile is not an incoherent profile.

Different moral traditions often support the same action, and one person may reasonably use different kinds of reasons in different settings. This tool reports recurring patterns. It flags only places where your selected action and selected explanation appear to point in different directions, or where a later rule conflicts with an earlier exception.

Method & limits

A reasoning profile, not a moral diagnosis

Each situation asks first what you would endorse and then which reason matters most. The reason—not the action by itself—adds a signal to the profile. The same action can be supported by consequences, rights, duties, care, character, fairness, loyalty, or authority.

A signal rate means “chosen out of the questions where this reason was offered.” It is not the chance that you belong to a theory, and it is not a moral score. Several strong signals indicate plural or context-sensitive reasoning, not automatic incoherence.

Moral realism and non-realism answer a different question from consequentialism, deontology, care ethics, or virtue ethics. The final metaethical answer is therefore reported separately and never raises or lowers a reasoning tendency.

A potential tension appears only when a chosen reason does not readily support the chosen action, or when a later rule conflicts with an earlier identity-based exception. Such a flag asks for another explanation; it does not prove the user is irrational.
