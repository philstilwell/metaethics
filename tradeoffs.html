Does “cannot be put on one scale” mean “anything goes”?

No. A comparison can use protected boundaries, priorities, several distinct reasons, or a fair procedure. The burden is to say what disciplines the decision and who has to live with it.

The main lesson

Hard comparison needs an explicit rule, not a hidden conversion rate.

Totals, protections, thresholds, priorities, plural judgment, fair procedures, and chosen settlements answer different parts of the problem. A mature account states which method acts first, what triggers the next, and what remains a genuine loss.
